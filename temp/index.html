CryptoCheckout MVP Blueprint

Non-custodial USDC checkout for ecommerce merchants.

You are building a payment verification layer, not a bank. Merchants create checkout links, customers pay USDC directly to the merchant wallet, your system detects the blockchain transaction, marks the checkout as paid, and sends a webhook to the merchant's store.

Exactly what you are building

Product: Stablecoin checkout links + merchant dashboard.
Network: Base first.
Token: USDC first. USDT later.
Settlement: Direct to merchant wallet.
Your role: Verify payment, record it, notify merchant.
Not MVP: Custody, fiat conversion, card payments, multi-chain chaos.

The strategic MVP

1

Merchant creates checkout

The merchant enters amount, currency, success URL, cancel URL, and order metadata. Your app returns a public checkout link.

2

Customer pays USDC

The checkout page shows the merchant wallet, exact amount, Base network, QR code, and payment status.

3

Worker verifies payment

A Railway worker watches USDC Transfer events, matches them to pending checkouts, updates Postgres, and triggers webhooks.

Visual architecture

🛒 Customer

Opens checkout link, connects wallet or scans QR code, sends exact USDC amount on Base.

🏪 Merchant

Creates checkouts, receives direct wallet settlement, configures webhooks, views payments.

▲ Next.js Web App

Landing page, dashboard, checkout pages, API routes, merchant auth, checkout creation, payment status API.

⚙ Railway Worker

Long-running TypeScript process. Watches blockchain events, matches payments, sends webhook jobs.

🐘 Railway Postgres

Source of truth for merchants, wallets, checkout sessions, payments, API keys, webhooks, and audit logs.

🔗 Base RPC Provider

Alchemy, QuickNode, Coinbase, or another RPC provider used by the worker to monitor USDC transfers.

📮 Merchant Store

Receives signed webhook such as checkout.paid and marks the ecommerce order as paid.

Payment flow

1

Create checkout

Merchant calls POST /api/checkouts or uses dashboard.

2

Open pay page

Customer sees amount, wallet address, network, and QR code.

3

Send USDC

Customer sends exact USDC on Base to merchant wallet.

4

Detect event

Worker listens for ERC-20 Transfer events.

5

Match payment

Recipient, amount, token contract, chain, and expiry are verified.

6

Notify store

Checkout becomes paid. Signed webhook is sent to merchant.

Build boundaries

✓

Build now

Checkout links, USDC/Base payments, direct merchant wallet settlement, payment detection, webhooks, dashboard.

→

Build after validation

WooCommerce plugin, Shopify app, store directory, subscriptions, smart contract invoice router, analytics.

×

Avoid early

Custody, fiat settlement, automatic conversion, card payments, multi-chain expansion, complex refunds.

Core rule: In the MVP, CryptoCheckout does not hold funds. It only provides checkout UX, blockchain verification, payment records, and signed merchant notifications.
